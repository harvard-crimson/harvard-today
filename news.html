In the News
Members of HUCTW negotiate mo' money: Whether there will also be "Mo' Problems" remains unclear, but members of Harvard's largest employee union, the Harvard Union of Clerical and Technical Workers, were happy to hear the details of significant changes to health care benefits and wage compensation after many months of negotiations.

GSAS shows some love to parents: Graduate School of Arts and Sciences students who are expecting children can now also count on more time off and a larger stipend. The change doubled the amount of leave time for students who are expecting, and added a $6,264 stipend. Ahh, the miracles (and economic burdens) of childbirth.

Law school remembers Scalia: Over this past weekend Law School alumnus and Supreme Court Justice Antonin G. Scalia passed away, leaving a vacant spot on the Supreme Court. Famous for his conservative approach to Constitutional Law, Scalia was remembered for his sharp wit, creativity, and adorable friendship with fellow Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg.

One Flyby writer made the questionable decision to walk to the Quad in -30 windchill. He lived to tell the tale, but only barely.

Oooh, you're my best friend: The Queen song may have been written about their bassist's wife, but it also may have been written about Conan O'Brien and a Crimson staff writer who met him on Friday. Yeah, that's probably it.
